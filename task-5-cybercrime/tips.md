
Stay Safe Online: A Student’s Guide (India Edition)
Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Always use an authenticator app or SMS codes for your University ERP, Gmail, and social media accounts.

Verify "Urgent" Requests: If a friend or professor asks for money or OTPs via WhatsApp, call them on a normal cellular call first to verify it’s really them.

Check the URL (HTTPS): Before entering credentials on any "Student Login" page, ensure the URL starts with https:// and the domain name is spelled correctly (e.g., google.com not g00gle.com).

Public Wi-Fi Hygiene: Avoid accessing your bank account or sensitive student portals while connected to free railway or cafe Wi-Fi; use your mobile data instead.

Audit App Permissions: Regularly check your phone settings to see which apps have unnecessary access to your "Contacts," "Microphone," or "Location."

Secure Your SIM: Set a SIM card PIN to prevent hackers from receiving your OTPs if your phone is stolen or your SIM is cloned.

UPI Safety (No PIN for Receiving): Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money. Scammers often send "Collect Requests" disguised as payments to you.

Verify QR Codes: Before scanning a QR code at a local vendor, ensure there isn't a fake sticker pasted over the original one. Check the name displayed in your UPI app before hitting 'Pay.'

 How to Report Cybercrime in India
If you or someone you know is a victim of a digital scam:

Immediate Action: Call the National Helpline at 1930.

Official Portal: File a formal complaint at cybercrime.gov.in.

Bank Contact: Notify your bank within 3 hours of a fraudulent transaction to increase the chances of a "reversal."
